## PRIMARY COLLECTION

### Root 1: Emotional / Relational > Relational Imprint > Fear > Fear of Abandonment > Prenatal / Early Life > In Utero
The deepest root. Gabi carries an imprint from in utero — a moment or season during the pregnancy where abandonment was in the field. This could have been uncertainty about the pregnancy, relational stress between parents, fear about the future, financial worry, anything that created a felt sense of "I might not be kept. I might not be wanted." The developing nervous system registered this not as a thought but as a felt reality. It had been dormant or expressing through other channels (the unmet-expectations pattern from Feb 19 may have been a safer way for this deeper root to express). Today's episode — the physical barrier of the gate, the locked door, the brief but total separation — created the exact conditions for this imprint to surface in its raw form.

### Root 2: Cognitive / Belief-Based > Limiting Belief > Worth Beliefs > "My value is conditional" > Prenatal / Early Life > In Utero
The belief that crystallized around the abandonment imprint. "You never wanted me" is not a present-tense observation — it's a declaration of a fixed truth about herself. I am the kind of person who is not wanted. My value depends on someone choosing to stay. If you leave — even to the bathroom — it confirms what I already know. This belief was installed prenatally and has been looking for confirmation ever since.

### Root 3: Relational Imprints > Attachment Pattern > Attachment Strategies > Anxious Attachment > Inherited > Maternal Line
A generational pattern of anxious attachment passed through the maternal line. This is the template that the prenatal imprint (Root 1) landed on. The abandonment fear found a ready-made structure — "this is how we respond to separation" — already present in the family line. This is why the response was so articulate, so specific, so disproportionate to the actual event.

### Root 4: Spiritual / Meaning / Authority > Spiritual Identity > Identity Formation > False Identity > Prenatal / Early Life > In Utero
Gabi received a false identity in utero: "I am the unwanted one." This is not who she is. But it has been operating as though it were true — a lens through which certain experiences get interpreted. The false identity gives the abandonment fear its authority: it's not just fear, it's "the truth about who I am."

## RESTORATION

### Root 1: Fear of Abandonment (Prenatal / In Utero)
- **Readiness:** Yes. Tender, but ready. The fact that it surfaced so clearly today suggests it was ready to be seen.
- **Authority Transfer (Prayer):** Father, this fear is older than Gabi's first breath. It was written into her body before she was born — a moment in the womb where she felt the question: Am I wanted? Will I be kept? She has been carrying the answer "no" ever since, and it is a lie. Lord, I ask You to go back to that moment — You were there, You formed her in the secret place, You saw every unformed day. Speak to the Gabi who was being knit together: You were always wanted. You were always Mine. Before your mother knew you, I knew you. Before the circumstances settled, your place was secure. Heal the imprint — not just comfort over it — heal it at the root. Where her developing nervous system encoded "unwanted," overwrite it with the truth: wanted, chosen, kept. Let the felt sense of being unwanted dissolve, and let the felt sense of belonging take its place. In Jesus' name.
- **Completion:** Deep. Something very old shifted. Image of a tiny curled form being held — not just comforted but claimed. The word: "settled." Not fully complete — deep root, may need second pass — but the acute charge has broken. The lie lost its authority.

### Root 2: "My value is conditional" (Prenatal / In Utero)
- **Readiness:** Ready. Flows directly from Root 1.
- **Authority Transfer (Prayer):** God, Gabi's heart learned before she was born that her value was conditional — that she is only worth something if someone chooses to stay, chooses to want her. I ask You to uproot this belief at its origin. Replace it with the truth that her value is not conditional. She is not valuable because she is wanted — she is wanted because she is valuable. Her worth was set by You, at conception, and no one's presence or absence changes it. Teach her heart — at the deep, pre-verbal level where this belief lives — that she is unconditionally valued. That she does not have to earn staying. That proximity is not proof of love, and distance is not proof of rejection. That she is held even when she cannot see the one holding her. In Jesus' name.
- **Completion:** Clean release. The belief didn't fight — it had been waiting to be corrected. Sense of something recalibrating below thought. Complete.

### Root 3: Anxious Attachment (Inherited / Maternal Line)
- **Readiness:** Some resistance. This pattern has been protective — generations of women alert to relational danger. Releasing it feels like losing a warning system. Willingness underneath the resistance.
- **Authority Transfer (Prayer):** Father, this anxious attachment pattern has been passed down through the maternal line — mother to daughter, generation after generation. A way of relating that says: If I don't hold on tight, I will be left. If I don't watch carefully, love will disappear. It was adaptive once. But it is not Gabi's to carry. Lord, break this generational pattern at the root. Not just in Gabi, but in the line. Where anxious attachment has been the inheritance, let secure attachment be the new legacy. Sever the cord that passes this pattern from mother to daughter. Replace hypervigilance with peace. Replace clutching with open hands. Replace "I must make sure they stay" with "I am safe whether they are in the room or not." And Lord — where this pattern also lives in Stephanie, address it gently in her too. Not as condemnation but as freedom. In Jesus' name.
- **Completion:** Something breaking — not violently, but like a chain link opening. Resistance melted midway. Not fully clear whether the generational component resolved completely or just Gabi's expression of it. Seeds planted in the line. Gabi's expression: resolved.

### Root 4: False Identity — "I am the unwanted one" (Prenatal / In Utero)
- **Readiness:** Ready. Other roots clearing made space. False identity now exposed without supporting structure.
- **Authority Transfer (Prayer):** God, Gabi has been wearing a name that is not hers: Unwanted. She has been carrying an identity formed in the dark, before birth, before she could know the truth about who she is. And today it spoke through her mouth: "You never wanted me." I ask You to remove this false identity. Strip it off her like a garment that was never hers. And speak her true name over her — the one You gave her before the foundation of the world. She is not the unwanted one. She is the chosen one. She is the daughter who was dreamed of, prayed for, delighted in. She is the one whose days were written in Your book before one of them came to be. Where "unwanted" has been her secret name, replace it with Beloved. Wanted. Kept. Delighted in. Let this new identity settle into the same deep place the old one lived — below words, below thought, in the body, in the bones. In Jesus' name.
- **Completion:** Strongest completion of the four. Something lit up. Image of a name tag being removed and a new one being written — not in ink but in light. The false identity didn't just release — it was replaced. Complete.
