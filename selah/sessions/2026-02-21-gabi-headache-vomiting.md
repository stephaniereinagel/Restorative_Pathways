## PRIMARY COLLECTION

### Root 1: Physical / Biological > Microbiological > Viral > Acute > Systemic (id: `e059f0c7c057`)
This reads most like an **acute systemic illness load** rather than a purely local or structural problem. Vomiting + headache often travels with a viral picture (or a short-lived “bug”) even when other details are unknown.

### Root 2: Physical / Biological > Chemical / Nutritional > Hydration > Hydration Status > Cellular Dehydration (id: `0ce468ff0209`)
Vomiting depletes fluid quickly in a small body. Headache can be a dehydration signal. This is not an accusation of “not enough water” — it’s simply the body’s present-state signal.

### Root 3: Physical / Biological > Chemical / Nutritional > Metabolic Regulation > Inflammation > Acute Inflammation (id: `539604548fab`)
An acute immune/inflammatory surge often accompanies a viral load and can amplify head pain and nausea/vomiting.

### Root 4: Nervous System / Stress Regulation > Acute Pattern > Fight Response > Hyperarousal > Personal Experience > Single Event (id: `a5db4f13418c`)
Sickness can spike a child’s system into **acute hyperarousal** (especially in a body that already has a known “0-to-100” profile). This doesn’t mean the illness is “just nervous system” — it means the illness can recruit the nervous system.

### Root 5: Somatic Patterning > Protective Body Response > Autonomic Regulation Patterns > Sympathetic Dominance > Personal Experience > Single Event (id: `6dbb98cd97de`)
An acute sympathetic dominance pattern can intensify pain perception, increase nausea, and make settling difficult. This root often resolves cleanly once the acute threat is recognized and the system is allowed to downshift.

## POST-SESSION NOTES

This session treated the presentation as **primarily physical/biological** with supportive nervous-system and autonomic regulation work so the body can rest and recover.

**Watch for:**
- Ability to keep down small sips / gradual return of appetite
- Urination/tears/energy trending back toward normal (dehydration signal reduction)
- Headache intensity decreasing as hydration and inflammation settle
- Whether hyperarousal patterns (panic, agitation, 0-to-100) soften during discomfort

**Medical-safety note (non-exhaustive):** If headache + vomiting is severe, persistent, accompanied by dehydration signs, high fever/stiff neck, unusual sleepiness/confusion, severe abdominal pain, or any “this is not normal for her” intuition — consider urgent medical evaluation.
